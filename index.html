EXPLORING AUSTRALIA'S FORESTS

Visualizing the Plantation Distribution and Economic Trends in Australia's Forest Industry

These visualizations provide an in-depth look at the distribution and economic value of Australia’s forest industry. By exploring the forest areas across states, the visualizations highlight the differences between hardwood and softwood plantations and their development over time.

They also showcase the economic contributions of various states through log volumes and sales income, offering insights into how forestry practices vary regionally. With interactive features such as year filters and tooltips, the charts allow users to explore trends in the forestry sector.

This map highlights the financial contribution of each Australian state to the forestry industry by showing their sales and service income. The data emphasizes how certain regions, like New South Wales and Victoria, play a major role in the economy, while others contribute more modestly. Understanding these regional differences helps paint a clearer picture of the industry’s economic landscape and its distribution across Australia.

The time series chart tracks the growth of softwood and hardwood plantations across Australia. By comparing the expansion of these two key forest types, we can observe how regional preferences and environmental factors shape the development of forestry. The growth patterns shown here reveal the long-term trends in forest area expansion, reflecting each state’s strategic decisions in balancing economic gain and sustainability.

This chart demonstrates the changing volume of logs harvested across different Australian states. It provides insight into each region's contribution to the overall log production, helping us understand which areas are key players in the supply of timber products. Tracking the changes over time highlights how the demand for forest products and state policies have influenced harvesting activities.

This bubble chart presents a comparative view of forest area, log volume, and the economic value of forestry in each state. By exploring the relationship between forest size and log value, we gain an understanding of how efficiently each state manages its forestry resources. The size of each bubble reflects the forest area, and comparing these across states sheds light on regional approaches to forest management.

This chart focuses on the financial value of logs harvested in each state, highlighting how different regions contribute to the national forestry economy. The data underscores the significance of regions like New South Wales and Victoria in log harvesting, while also showing the contributions of smaller states. This helps illustrate the balance between economic production and forest sustainability efforts across Australia.
